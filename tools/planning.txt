Goal: Build a mobile-friendly trigram labeling tool for my internal use.

Purpose:
Speed up labeling trigrams for my weekly word game.

Current workflow:

* I have a list of all possible trigrams in a Google Sheet ("possible" means all trigrams generated by tools/corpus/all_trigrams_4_to_15.py and outputted into data/corpus/all_trigrams_4_to_15.txt)
* There are 1900 trigrams total
* But not all 1900 would make for a fun/interesting game (e.g. a trigram like "CCE" might technically have scrabble-valid words of length 4 through 15, but a lot are esoteric words and not something I can reasonably expect a player to come up with)
* So I manually run get_words(trigram) on each trigram, and label it YES/NO/MAYBE based on whether I think it's a good candidate for the game. This is a bit tedious, especially since I have to switch back and forth between the Google Sheet and my terminal where I run get_words.
* Each week, I choose which trigram to use for the game
* The choice is influenced by prior/recent trigrams used for the game. Like one week's trigram shouldn't be too similar to the previous 2-3 weeks trigrams (or yield lots of similar word options), and I want to make sure I have a good mix of "easy" vs "hard" trigrams over time. And a good variety of core vowels (not often consecutive trigrams with an "A" for instance) and a good mix of first letter (not too many trigrams starting with "I" when I haven't yet used trigrams starting with other letters). I imagine there's a histogram of total viable trigrams per each starter letter, and per each core vowel(s), etc., and my mix of trigrams used each week should reflect that distribution, but because so far ive only labelled like 20% of the trigrams I'm flying a bit blind and might not be operating in a way that's reflective of the overall distribution.
* So each week I roughly look at the last 4-5 weeks of trigrams (it's a weekly game so this is just 4-5 total trigrams), and then I look through my Google Sheet to find a good candidate for the next trigram, starting by looking at the trigrams labelled YES, and if none of those fit the trigram-shape I'm looking for then I search through the unlabelled trigram and run get_words.py to confirm a trigram is viable/fun for the game before selecting it. This is also a bit tedious, especially since I have to switch back and forth between the Google Sheet and my terminal where I run get_words.
* When I choose a trigram I run tools/add_new_trigram.sh to do things like add the trigram to data/trigram_calendar.jso, and I label the trigram as DONE in the google sheet (instead of YES/NO/MAYBE). So "DONE" means used in production.

Desired workflow:

* Open a mobile-friendly page/a[[]]
* See one unlabeled trigram at a time
* Automatically show get_words output
* Tap YES / NO / MAYBE / SKIP (and optional add-comment)
* Persist label/comment back to storage
* Auto-load next trigram (almost like swiping through tinder)
* Option to search for specific trigram and label it on-demand (for when I'm looking for a specific trigram to use for the next game, and want to quickly label it without having to scroll through the Google Sheet and then switch to terminal to run get_words)
* Place to view all trigrams i labelled with this tool and their labels/comments (in case I want to review / edit a decision)
* Ability to view all labelled trigrams and all unlabeled trigrams (in case I want to get a sense of the overall distribution of labelled vs unlabelled trigrams, and the distribution of YES/NO/MAYBE labels, and how much work i have left, etc.)
* Place to view the actual trigram calendar (data/trigram_calendar.json) to see the exact sequence of trigrams I've used in production so far

Constraints:

* Mobile-friendly, something I can easily do while watching tv or something


Questions to decide:

* Google Sheet vs JSON/DB storage?
* Local-only vs deployed?
* Should this live in trigram folder/repo (and perhaps navigable-to via trigram.netlify.app/[something]), or be a separate repo/tool?
* Whether to precompute get_words or generate live/on-demand?
